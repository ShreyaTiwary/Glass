👋 About Me

I am a PhD researcher working at the intersection of chemistry, physics, and computation. My research focuses on the temperature-dependent behavior of glass and nanoparticles.

I use molecular simulations and coding (Python, Fortran, Bash) to study systems that are otherwise difficult to capture through experiments.

Outside research, I enjoy anime, The Office (on a near-daily loop for the past seven years), and occasionally overthinking my life choices.

🔬 Research

Focus Areas:

  • Temperature-dependent glassy dynamics
  • Structural relaxation in amorphous systems
  • Nanoparticle interactions in glassy matrices

Tools: LAMMPS, GROMACS, Python, Fortran, Bash

Glass looks solid, but behaves like a very slow liquid. I study how it changes with temperature—and why it sometimes acts unpredictably.

Glass doesn’t forget its past, and I study why.

🚀 Current Work

Exploring temperature-dependent structural relaxation in glass systems and how microscopic dynamics evolve with cooling.
